How I Work

Design Process

01
Problem Framing
Start with user behaviour data, not assumptions. Map the job-to-be-done.
02
Systems Thinking
Map the full ecosystem before designing a single screen.
03
Decision Making
Every design decision has a hypothesis. Hypotheses get validated.
04
Trade-offs
Speed vs depth. Delight vs compliance. I name the trade-off and choose deliberately.
Start with the behaviour, not the brief.

Before opening Figma, I analyse session recordings, funnel data, and support logs. I want to know where users fail and why — not just what the stakeholder thinks the problem is. Good problem framing cuts weeks of iteration.

User Research & Behaviour Analysis
The screen is the last thing I design.

Financial products are systems. I map user journeys, service touchpoints, and technical constraints before touching component layout. This prevents designing beautiful dead-ends that don't survive implementation.

System Mapping & Flow Architecture
Decisions anchored to measurable outcomes.

Every design choice I make has a stated hypothesis: "If we reduce the KYC steps from 5 to 3, completion will increase by 10%+." I test assumptions early and cheaply, then iterate. No HiPPO decisions.

Hypothesis-Driven Design
Name the trade-off. Own the decision.

FinTech design is full of genuine tensions: security vs. convenience, regulatory compliance vs. user delight, speed to market vs. design quality. I make trade-offs explicit, document them, and take ownership of the consequences.
